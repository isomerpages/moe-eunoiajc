<h4><strong>Eligibility and Admission Criteria</strong></h4>
<p>The DSA-JC exercise is open to all <strong>Secondary 4 students in local schools</strong> who
demonstrate special talents and achievements in areas such as sports, the
arts, bilingualism, music, humanities, or leadership. Applicants should
be confident in their ability to handle the academic rigour of the A-Level
curriculum at Eunoia Junior College.</p>
<p>Successful applicants must:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Show strong passion and aptitude in their chosen DSA domain;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Possess a clear understanding of the requirements of a junior college
education;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Be familiar with the subject combinations offered at EJC</strong> and
ensure alignment with their academic goals.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>Students offered a place through DSA will receive a <strong>Letter of Offer by 12 August 2025</strong>.
Those placed on the waiting list will be notified via phone, and all other
applicants will be informed of their application outcome via email.</p>
<p>Successful applicants must confirm acceptance of the offer by <strong>15 August 2025, 5pm</strong>.
Applicants on the waiting list will be informed of their final outcome
by the same deadline.</p>
<p>Please note:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Successful DSA-JC applicants will not be allowed to participate in the
Joint Admissions Exercise (JAE), as a place in the college would have been
reserved for them.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>They are also not permitted to transfer to another junior college after
the release of the GCE O-Level results.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Students are expected to honour their DSA commitment by actively participating
in activities related to the talent area they were admitted under, for
the full duration of the programme.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Students who do not fulfil their DSA obligations without valid reason
may be required to transfer to another school.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>For more information, please visit the Ministry of Education <a href="https://www.moe.gov.sg/post-secondary/admissions/dsa" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"><u>DSA-JC website</u></a>.</p>
<p></p>
<h3><strong>Application Process </strong></h3>
<h4><strong>Submission of Supporting Documents</strong></h4>
<p>Applicants are required to submit the necessary documents, including academic
results and supporting materials that are required in the various categories
as stated below in their chosen domain.&nbsp;</p>
<p></p>
<p>All applicants must complete the <a href="https://form.gov.sg/6814c2ee4ce70c8000c4c68b" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"><u>online application form</u></a> and
submit it by 30 June 2025. They should also upload the required supporting
documents and/or videos for their CCA or programme into a publicly accessible
Google Drive folder and include the folder link in the form. <strong>We strongly advise applicants to carefully read the submission requirements for their selected CCA or programme (under “Criteria And Selection Process”) before filling in the online application form.</strong>
</p>
<p></p>
<h4><strong>Programme-Specific Trial/Audition/Selection Exercise</strong></h4>
<p>Shortlisted applicants will be invited to participate in programme-specific
trials, assessments or auditions to evaluate their current skill level,
potential, and readiness for the demands of the programme.</p>
<p></p>
<p><strong>Interview</strong>
</p>
<p>Applicants will attend an interview that will assess their:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Readiness for the academic and co-curricular demands of junior college
education.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Readiness, passion and commitment towards the domain in which they are
applying.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Understanding of and dedication to fulfilling the expectations of the
DSA programme.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><strong>Criteria And Selection Process</strong>
</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Sports CCAs</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Performing Arts CCAs</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Bilingualism</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Music Elective Programme (MEP)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Humanities Programme</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Leadership</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<h4><strong>Sports CCAs</strong></h4>
<p><strong>Phase 1: Shortlisting</strong>
</p>
<p>Applicants are required to consolidate and submit the necessary documents
and supporting materials through a Google Drive link in the <a href="https://form.gov.sg/6814c2ee4ce70c8000c4c68b" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"><u>online application form</u></a>,
demonstrating passion and strong ability in their niche area:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>One set of Secondary 3 End-of-Year examination results and one set of
Secondary 4 Weighted Assessment results.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p></p>
<p><strong>Phase 2: Physical Trial</strong>
</p>
<p>Applicants will be required to attend a physical trial/selection exercise.
You will be contacted by the CCA teacher in charge of the category that
you are applying for. Interviews will be conducted in June/July 2025, exact
details will be emailed/SMSed to successful applicants.</p>
<p><strong>Phase 3: Interview</strong>
</p>
<p>Applicants will be assessed for their suitability and readiness for JC
curriculum and alignment with school values.&nbsp;</p>
<p></p>
<h4><strong>Performing Arts CCAs</strong></h4>
<p><strong>Phase 1: Shortlisting</strong>
</p>
<p>Applicants are required to consolidate the following items and submit
them through a Google Drive link in the <strong><a href="https://form.gov.sg/6814c2ee4ce70c8000c4c68b" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"><u>online application form</u></a></strong>:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>One set of Secondary 3 End-of-Year examination results and one set of
Secondary 4 Weighted Assessment results.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>A video recording of solo/small group performance (dance or music) showing
current standard with the following requirements:</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Recorded within the last two years.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Applicant’s face, hands, and full body (for dance) must be visible.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>No editing or enhancements allowed.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Certificates of achievement in relevant international or national competitions
other than SYF (if any).</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Additional qualifications if any (e.g. ABRSM certificates, dance certifications).</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Phase 2: Audition</strong>
</p>
<p>Shortlisted applicants will attend a live audition to evaluate their skill
set and artistic potential.</p>
<p><strong>Phase 3: Interview</strong>
</p>
<p>Applicants will be assessed for their suitability and readiness for JC
curriculum and alignment with school values.&nbsp;</p>
<p></p>
<h4><strong>Bilingualism&nbsp;</strong></h4>
<p><strong>Shortlisting Criteria</strong>
</p>
<p>Applicants are required to demonstrate passion and strong ability in both
English and Chinese. They must meet one or more of the following:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Applicants should have consistently obtained distinctions in O-Level English
and either Chinese or Higher Chinese.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>They should also demonstrate strong bilingual proficiency and a keen interest
in both Western and Chinese cultures, as evidenced by achievements or awards
in language-related competitions, essay writing, or project work.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Successful candidates are expected to participate actively in the Bicultural
Studies Programme and must offer at least one of the following H2 subjects:
China Studies in Chinese, Chinese Language and Literature, or Translation.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Selection Exercise&nbsp;</strong>
</p>
<p>Applicants are required to consolidate the following items and submit
them through a Google Drive link in the <a href="https://form.gov.sg/6814c2ee4ce70c8000c4c68b" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"><u>online application form</u></a><strong>:</strong>
</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>One set of Secondary 3 End-of-Year examination results and one set of
Secondary 4 Weighted Assessment results.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Two pieces of writing—one in English and one in Chinese—must be submitted
(school essays are acceptable).</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>A video in Mandarin (not exceeding 2 minutes) on the topic: "How I can
contribute as a DSA Bilingualism student." The video should be recorded
in landscape orientation and submitted in MP4 format.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Interview</strong>
</p>
<p>Shortlisted applicants will be required to attend an interview to assess
their proficiency in both languages, appreciation of Chinese and Western
cultures, and potential to apply their bilingual and bicultural strengths
meaningfully during their time in junior college and in future endeavours.</p>
<p></p>
<h4><strong>Music Elective Programme (MEP)&nbsp;</strong></h4>
<p><strong>Shortlisting Criteria</strong>
</p>
<p>Applicants are required to demonstrate passion and strong ability in music
and meet one or more of the following:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Quality grades in O-Level Music or Higher Music.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Achievements/awards in music performance or composition in international
or national competitions or events.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Participation in national level ensembles (e.g., SNYO, SNYCO).</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Selection exercise through video and composition/arrangement submission</strong>
</p>
<p>Applicants are required to consolidate the following items and submit
them through a Google Drive link in the <a href="https://form.gov.sg/6814c2ee4ce70c8000c4c68b" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"><u>online application form</u></a><strong>:</strong>
</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>One set of Secondary 3 End-of-Year examination results and one set of
Secondary 4 Weighted Assessment results.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Video recordings of two contrasting pieces, 5–10 minutes total (recorded
within the last 2 years).</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>One original composition/arrangement (2–5 minutes), submitted as:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Acoustic: Score (PDF) + demo audio (MP3/WAV), <strong>OR</strong>
</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>DAW-produced: Exported audio track only (MP3/WAV).</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Interview and selection test&nbsp;</strong>
</p>
<p>Shortlisted applicants are required to attend an interview and selection
test to evaluate their listening and creative skill, theoretical understanding
and ability to meet demands of the MEP curriculum.&nbsp;</p>
<p></p>
<h4><strong>Humanities Programme&nbsp;</strong></h4>
<p><strong>Shortlisting Criteria</strong>
</p>
<p>Applicants must have consistently strong academic performance, particularly
in the humanities and English Language. They must meet one or more of the
following:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Distinctions in O-Level English Language and at least one humanities subject.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Recognised achievements in competitions, essays, or projects in humanities.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Evidence of a broad and balanced reading diet.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>A strong and nuanced interest in global and local issues.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Ability to articulate a unique personal perspective.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Selection Exercise&nbsp;</strong>
</p>
<p>Applicants are required to consolidate the following items and submit
them through a Google Drive link in the <a href="https://form.gov.sg/6814c2ee4ce70c8000c4c68b" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"><u>online application form</u></a><strong>:</strong>
</p>
<p>i) &nbsp; &nbsp; One set of Secondary 3 End-of-Year examination results
and one set of Secondary 4 Weighted Assessment results.</p>
<p>ii) Two samples of original written work: school essays may be included.</p>
<p>iii) Critical thinking essay: keep within 600 words.</p>
<p>EITHER&nbsp;</p>
<p>Based on your own experiences, why are the humanities important for you
and for society, and what have you done so far to spark interest in the
humanities among those around you?</p>
<p>OR</p>
<p>What is your own insight into a recent and significant local or international
event that you feel shows the enduring value of the humanities?</p>
<p><strong>Interview</strong>
</p>
<p>Shortlisted applicants are required to attend an interview to assess analytical
depth, originality of thought, and ability to contribute meaningfully to
the programme.</p>
<p></p>
<h4><strong>Leadership&nbsp;</strong></h4>
<p><strong>Shortlisting Criteria</strong>
</p>
<p>Applicants will be assessed based on their leadership beliefs and leadership
potential. We are looking for applicants who exhibit qualities encapsulated
in our college vision:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Youth with Purpose</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Thinker with Heart</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Leader with Courage</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Selection Exercise&nbsp;</strong>
</p>
<p>Applicants are required to consolidate the following items and submit
them through a Google Drive link in the <a href="https://form.gov.sg/6814c2ee4ce70c8000c4c68b" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank"><u>online application form</u></a><strong>:</strong>
</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>One set of Secondary 3 End-of-Year examination results and one set of
Secondary 4 Weighted Assessment results.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>A maximum 3-minute video (there is no need for special effects and visual
embellishments): "Based on your personal experiences, what does it mean
to lead?" <em>(Please note that applicants need not have held formal leadership roles but should be able to articulate who you are and what leadership means to you.)</em>
</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>A document containing the name, designation and contact details of two
referees who can vouch for your credibility.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Interview</strong>
</p>
<p>Shortlisted applicants are required to attend a group interview for the
panel to evaluate your leadership potential, interpersonal skills, and
alignment of qualities with EJC’s college vision.</p>
