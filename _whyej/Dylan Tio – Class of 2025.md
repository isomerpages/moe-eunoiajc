<p>I am <strong>Dylan Tio</strong> (24-O5), Deputy President of the EJC Choir,
and a Music Elective Program student who also studies Physics, Chemistry
and Mathematics. I had experienced the vibrant culture of EJ right when
I first entered as a fresh JC1 student, and the “work hard, play hard”
mindset really resonated with me. Here, I have had the opportunities to
step out of my comfort zone, be bolder, and explore who I want to be.</p>
<p>The three biggest things that inspired and motivated me in EJ were my
friends, family and — this is going to sound a bit strange — my personal
failures. When I first joined EJ, the rigours of JC life were quite a change
for me, and I faced quite a few unexpected failures in academic and other
areas at the start. This motivated me to push forward and never give up.&nbsp;</p>
<p>I am grateful for the support I received from my teachers, who always
answered my questions and were willing to meet me for consultations. Throughout
all this, my family and friends were the pillar of support I could always
count on. Thank you to 24-O5, EJChoir, MEP ‘24 and to everyone else who
made my EJC journey that much more special!</p>
<p>There are so many unforgettable experiences to capture in this reflection,
that I think I would be better off describing a feeling instead. That feeling
would be awe — awe for my fellow Eunoians — their willingness to stay up
late to plan events, their resilience when they pull all-nighters in an
attempt to catch up with lectures after a long day of CCAs, their multiple
talents, and their fiery energy when participating in school events. I
learnt that there is truly so much to learn from each and every person
I meet and I’ll certainly hold my EJC experience close to my heart.</p>
