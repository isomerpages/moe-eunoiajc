<p><strong>Tay Karlyn</strong> (24-E2), Chairperson of EJ Heal Hearts and
Peer Support Leader, brought purpose and compassion to everything she did.
Her dedication to serving the community, whether through outreach projects
or mentoring her peers, helped shape her into a thoughtful and responsible
leader. Beyond her achievements, Karlyn is remembered for the warmth and
encouragement she offered, creating a sense of belonging that extended
far beyond her immediate circle.</p>
<p>“My EJ journey has completely changed the way I understand time. I used
to be skeptical when people said that these two years would pass in the
blink of an eye, but now that I’ve lived through it, I realise that that
description hardly does it justice. My time in EJ has been fulfilling,
joyful, and deeply impactful. It was a journey where I was able to explore
different roles, make mistakes, and grow from them, all while being supported
by friends and teachers every step of the way.</p>
<p>A large part of my experience was shaped by leading community service
projects. Working with the cancer community, people with disabilities,
and my fellow youth volunteers gave me a strong sense of purpose in all
that I did. I felt incredibly fortunate to be surrounded by like-minded
individuals who shared the same heart to serve. Through these experiences,
and with the guidance and constructive feedback from my teachers, I learned
how to become a more thoughtful and responsible leader — lessons I know
I will carry with me beyond EJ.</p>
<p>When I look back, what stands out most is the warmth I felt throughout
my journey. Each phase of JC, from orientation, CCA, to exams, seemed to
pass so quickly, often leaving me in disbelief at how fleeting those moments
were. Yet, within that brevity, I found genuine friendships, meaningful
experiences, and valuable lessons. More than anything, it is this sense
of belonging and the people I met along the way that I will always remember
of EJ.”</p>
